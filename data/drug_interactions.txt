DRUG SAFETY AND INTERACTION DATABASE

PARACETAMOL (Acetaminophen)
- Class: Analgesic/Antipyretic.
- Safety: Safe for most adults in labeled doses (max 4g/day).
- Interactions: avoid taking with alcohol (liver toxicity risk). No major interaction with antibiotics.
- Side Effects: Rare. Liver damage in overdose.

AMOXICILLIN
- Class: Antibiotic (Penicillin type).
- Safety: Safe if not allergic to penicillin.
- Interactions: potentially reduces effectiveness of oral contraceptives.
- Side Effects: Nausea, diarrhea, rash.

IBUPROFEN (Advil, Motrin)
- Class: NSAID.
- Safety: Use with caution in patients with stomach ulcers, kidney issues, or high blood pressure.
- Interactions:
  - ASPIRIN: Avoid combination (increases bleeding risk).
  - WARFARIN (Blood thinners): HIGH RISK. Do not combine (major bleeding risk).
  - Alcohol: Increases risk of stomach bleeding.
- Side Effects: Stomach pain, heartburn.

CETIRIZINE (Zyrtec)
- Class: Antihistamine.
- Safety: Non-drowsy (usually), but can cause drowsiness in some.
- Interactions: Avoid with alcohol or sedatives (increases drowsiness).
- Side Effects: Dry mouth, fatigue.

METFORMIN
- Class: Antidiabetic.
- Safety: Watch for lactic acidosis (rare).
- Interactions: Alcohol (increases lactic acidosis risk). Iodinated contrast dyes (stop before CT scans).

LISINOPRIL
- Class: ACE Inhibitor.
- Safety: Watch for dry cough.
- Interactions: Potassium supplements (risk of high potassium). NSAIDs (can reduce effectiveness).
